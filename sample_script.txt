SCENE 1
INT. COFFEE SHOP - DAY - RAINY

A cozy, dimly lit coffee shop. Rain streaks against the large front window. The sound of an espresso machine hisses in the background.

SARAH (30s, exhausted but alert) sits at a corner table, nursing a lukewarm cappuccino. She stares intently at a manila folder in front of her.

A bell chimes as the front door opens. 

MARK (40s, drenched, wearing a trench coat) steps inside. He shakes off his umbrella and scans the room. His eyes lock onto Sarah. He quickly makes his way over and slides into the booth opposite her.

MARK
(Breathless)
Did you bring it?

SARAH
(Without looking up)
You're late, Mark. 

MARK
Traffic was a nightmare. The bridge is completely flooded. 

Sarah finally looks up. Her eyes are sharp, calculating. She slides the manila folder across the table.

SARAH
Everything is in there. The blueprints, the schedules, the security codes. 

Mark opens the folder slightly, peeking inside. He nods, satisfied.

MARK
Are you sure about this? Once we cross this line, there's no going back.

SARAH
(Leaning in close)
I stopped caring about lines a long time ago. 

Mark closes the folder and tucks it inside his coat. He gives Sarah a long, hard look before standing up.

MARK
I'll contact you when it's done.

Mark turns and heads out into the rain, the door bell chiming a final time. Sarah picks up her cappuccino and takes a slow sip, a faint smile playing on her lips.

[SCENE END]
